## Mosaic's display tank parameters (authoritative reef-keeping baseline for macroalgae)

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Temperature | 72-78°F (22.2-25.6°C) |
| pH | 8.1-8.4 |

| Nitrite | 0 |
| Ammonia | 0 |

*Note: Mosaic doesn't publish kH or salinity targets in this guide. Use standard reef defaults (kH 8-12, SG 1.023-1.026).*

## Lighting

- Full-spectrum LED preferred
- Most freshwater-planted LEDs work for most species
- AVOID actinic-only spectrum — "cannot support many of the more difficult species"
- No specific PAR ranges given

## Flow

- Generally not critical
- Too much flow tears delicate species
- Soft-coral-tank-like conditions are appropriate (some movement, strong surface agitation)

## Attachment / Propagation (4 official methods)

1. **Superglue** — short-term temporary attachment of small cuttings
2. **Zip-ties / Fishing line** — "one of our favorites" (don't tie too tightly)
3. **Wedging** — "our favorite" — between rocks or other algae
4. **Sand bed** — ONLY for specific genera

**CRITICAL: Sand-bed acceptance by genus**:
- ✅ Suitable: Caulerpa, Halimeda, Penicillus, Rhipocephalus, Udotea (rhizoid-anchored or sand-substrate species)
- ❌ Will MELT at base: ALL red algae, ALL brown algae

This dramatically restricts substrate options for the bulk of the slice (~85% of entries are red/brown).

## Nutrients

- **Macronutrients**: kept moderately high (5-20 ppm nitrate, 0.02-0.1 ppm phosphate) — macroalgae feed on these
- **Micronutrients**: weekly dosing of iron + iodine + other trace elements
- Mosaic recommends an "all-around micronutrient fertilizer at least weekly"

## Care-level distinctions

The guide references "more difficult species" and "delicate species" — explicitly naming Halymenia and Nemastoma — but provides no formal easy/medium/hard system.

## Acclimation

No specific guidance. Standard reef live-arrival practices apply.
